  New Exchange Requirements May Affect Your Listing; Update Before Oct 16th  
  If you have an integration, add-on, or extension listed on the Adobe Exchange for Creative Cloud, please read on to find out how your listing(s) on the Exchange may be affected by a recent announcement regarding certain unauthorized software.

The list of unauthorized software was announced in May of 2019. Please refer to the official chart of unauthorized software here. To be clear, if the products you support are all authorized versions, you need not take action at this time.

Going forward, this will affect Exchange listings in the following ways.

  • As of September 6th, new listings are no longer allowed to list compatibility for unauthorized products.
  • Beginning on November 8th, 2019, compatibility with unauthorized versions of Creative Cloud apps will be automatically hidden from Exchange listings.
  • After November 8th, of 2019, listings that exclusively support unauthorized versions of software will be removed from the Exchange. Read our detailed article on the Adobe Tech Blog to find out how to update your listing to avoid removal.
  • Future listings may be rejected if they list compatibility exclusively with unauthorized products in the manifest.
  • Do you need to download an installer for an older, but still authorized Creative Cloud app? Read our instructions in the second half of this blog post, "What if my customers are not using the latest Creative Cloud apps?"

We have a detailed guide to updating your manifest on the Adobe Tech Blog. If you currently support unauthorized products, please update your listing before October 16th, 2019.

More documentation on manifests can be found in the CEP Cookbook. If you require assistance updating your manifest, please contact Exchange Support.

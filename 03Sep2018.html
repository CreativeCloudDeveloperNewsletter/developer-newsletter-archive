September 13th, 2018

Hello!

Welcome to the September issue of our Creative Cloud developer newsletter.

This month we have big news about Adobe XD APIs, some important changes to the Exchange developer program , information on how to beta test CEP 9, and new packaging guides to help you deploy your software.

Adobe XD API News

In August, we announced the companies and developers that will be the first to launch Adobe XD plugins and integrations. We even helped kickstart a few projects live and in person at a special three-day event.

If you want to be the first to find out when Adobe XD APIs go public, sign up here.

Adobe Exchange News

We had a big announcement last month on the changes to our Exchange developer program. Since then, we’ve published a lot of resources to help you get the most out of our new Exchange Program.

Check out the announcement on the Adobe Tech Blog. You can also watch our webinars on the new Exchange Program benefits and best practices for the Exchange Marketplace .

CEP News

Don’t forget, CEP 9 is on the way. Sign up now to test your extensions and panels on a prerelease.

We need more feedback from third party developers on CEP. Whether you’ve tested CEP 9 yet or not, please take our survey about CEP.

By the way, we recently cleaned and updated our CEP Github samples repo.

Packaging for Deployment

We listened to you, and discovered that packaging extensions was major pain point for our developers. We’ve got a bunch of updates for you to make packaging easier:

  • Check out our brand new packaging guide.
  • Read about common package customization questions.
  • Learn how to deploy your software, including at the enterprise level.
  • We now have ExManCMD 7.5 on the Creative Cloud Developer Prerelease.

Anything else we can do to help with packaging extensions? Let us know in our survey.

Did you miss the last newsletter? You can read it online here.

Thank you!
